The Material

What Controlled Low-Strength Material Is

Controlled low-strength material (CLSM) is a flowable, cement-based backfill that places itself — poured straight from the truck into a trench or void, it self-levels and self-compacts with no mechanical tamping. Once set, it forms a firm, settlement-resistant mass that supports the surface above without behaving like structural concrete.

The "controlled low-strength" part is the point: the mix is engineered to a deliberately low compressive strength so the fill can be dug back out by hand or backhoe years later when a utility needs servicing — without jackhammering. That makes CLSM the preferred alternative to compacted granular backfill in road cuts, congested corridors, and any trench where settlement or future access matters.

Ontario spec note: In Ontario, unshrinkable backfill / CLSM is specified under OPSS.MUNI 1359, with trench placement governed by OPSS.MUNI 401. OE supplies and places CLSM to the governing municipal specification on each job; the mix design and acceptance criteria are set by the Contract Administrator or the Customer's engineer.

Why OE

The Integrated Difference

Most suppliers drop the fill and leave. OE places the CLSM as one step in the same dig-and-restore job — the crew that opened the trench backfills and restores it, on the same contract, with one project lead.

That single-source model matters most on a live road cut: the excavation, the utility work, the CLSM backfill, and the surface restoration are sequenced by one team instead of waiting on a separate fill supplier to show up between trades. Fewer handoffs, fewer trucks on site, fewer days the lane is open.

Booking the excavation, CLSM backfill, and restoration together as one OE job is where the savings show up: up to 10% cost savings, 50% productivity gains, and a $40/hr discount when services are booked together — one mobilization, one crew, one point of accountability instead of waiting on a separate fill supplier. Because OE runs its own integrated operations across 6 Ontario locations, coordinating fewer trucks and trips cuts travel costs and emissions by 30%.
