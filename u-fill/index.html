What It Is

What Is Unshrinkable Fill (CLSM)?

Unshrinkable fill — also called flowable fill or controlled low-strength material (CLSM) — is a cement-based backfill delivered and placed straight from the truck.

It flows into the trench and self-levels, filling voids and irregular spaces no compactor can reach, then hardens to a stable, low-strength material. The strength is kept deliberately low so it can still be dug out by hand or machine if a buried line ever needs future access. Because it self-compacts, it removes the lift-by-lift compaction step granular backfill requires — eliminating the settlement and trench-failure callbacks that compaction errors cause.

Built to Ontario municipal spec: OE supplies and places unshrinkable fill to Ontario municipal specification (OPSS 1359 / TS 13.10) — the right mix design for the Customer's permit and reinstatement requirements.

Common Questions

Unshrinkable Fill (CLSM) FAQ

Answers to the questions OE's sales team hears most often.

What is unshrinkable fill / CLSM?
Unshrinkable fill — also called flowable fill or controlled low-strength material (CLSM) — is a cement-based backfill delivered and placed straight from the truck. It flows into the trench, self-levels, and hardens into a stable, low-strength material, filling voids and irregular spaces no compactor can reach.
How is it different from granular backfill?
Granular backfill has to be placed and mechanically compacted in lifts, and any compaction error shows up later as settlement and a failed trench. Unshrinkable fill self-compacts — it flows into place and fills every void on its own, removing the lift-by-lift compaction step and the settlement callbacks that come with it.
Can it be dug up later?
Yes. The strength is kept deliberately low so the material can still be excavated by hand or machine if a buried line ever needs future access — you get a stable, settlement-free backfill that doesn't lock the utility in permanently.
Does OE's mix meet Ontario municipal spec?
OE supplies and places unshrinkable fill to Ontario municipal specification (OPSS 1359 / TS 13.10), with the mix design matched to the Customer's permit and reinstatement requirements.
How soon can a trench be reopened to traffic?
Quick-set mixes are available to get busy corridors reopened sooner. Actual set and reopening time depends on the mix design, depth, and site conditions, so we confirm it for your specific job rather than promising a fixed time.
Why book U-Fill with OE rather than a separate supplier?
Because OE can dig, repair, backfill, and restore under one company, you get a single point of accountability instead of coordinating a separate concrete supplier and waiting on another vendor's truck. Booking the work together also unlocks OE's integrated-service bundling discount — one mobilization, one crew, one team responsible for the result.
