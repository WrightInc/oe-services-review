This report sets out OE Utility Services’ sustainability performance, practices, and forward commitments across four areas: Safety, Environment, People & Culture, and Governance.

Reporting Period

The 2025 Sustainability Report covers OE Utility Services’ performance and activities from January 1 to December 31, 2025, with reference to significant developments through the date of publication.

Scope and Boundaries

This report covers OE Utility Services’ operations in Ontario, Canada. OE operates from six locations: the Head Office and Main Yard in Vaughan, and regional yards in Hamilton, Durham (Courtice), Barrie (Holland Landing), London (Thames Centre), and Ottawa (Nepean). Data presented reflects the full operating footprint unless otherwise noted.

Where 2024 figures are referenced for comparison, the source is OE’s 2024 Sustainability Report. Where prior-year comparisons require clarification — for example, where the basis of measurement has changed — that change is noted alongside the data.

Data and Methodology

Operational figures in this report (training hours, audit counts, driver coaching sessions and outcomes, fleet size, Kaizen initiatives, supplier spend, and EcoVadis results) are drawn from OE’s internal management systems and the records of the responsible functional leaders.

The three headline waste-processing statistics (silt and clay diverted, sand processed, and water recycled) are reported from OE’s integrated soil recycling facility billing records. The 2025 sand figure reflects sand only and is not directly comparable to the 2024 figure, which included sand and aggregate combined.

OE is currently undertaking a comprehensive review of its greenhouse gas measurement methodology and baseline assumptions. Specific GHG performance figures are not reported in this document; an updated position will be provided in a future report once the review is complete.

Assurance

The content of this report has not been subject to independent third-party assurance. All figures and statements have been reviewed and confirmed by the OE leadership team prior to publication.

Forward-Looking Statements

This report contains forward-looking statements regarding OE Utility Services’ plans, commitments, and intentions, including but not limited to forward statements on Indigenous engagement, greenhouse gas measurement, fleet development, community investment, and operational growth. Forward-looking statements are based on assumptions and expectations that OE believes to be reasonable as of the date of publication. Actual outcomes may differ from those described. OE undertakes no obligation to update forward-looking statements except as required by applicable practice.

Reporting Cycle

OE Utility Services publishes a Sustainability Report annually. Prior reports remain accessible on the OE website at www.oeservices.ca/sustainability.

Message from the President & CEO

Message from the President and CEO

Keith Boulton, President and CEO

It is my privilege to introduce OE Utility Services’ 2025 Sustainability Report — my first as President and CEO. Joining OE has reinforced what I believed coming in: that this company’s strength is its people, and that sustainability, done honestly, is the natural outcome of putting people, safety, and craft at the centre of the business.

Sustainability is not a department at OE. It is the result of thousands of small, disciplined decisions made every day — in the field, in the yard, in the office, and at the leadership table. This report tells the story of those decisions in 2025.

Our commitment is to build a company our employees are proud of, our Customers can rely on, and our communities benefit from. That is the work.

That commitment shows up most directly in how we handle the material we collect. OE disposes only at Environmental Compliance Approval (ECA) sites, and the bulk of our hydrovac waste is recycled at our integrated soil recycling facility, where it is transformed into saleable products — clean fill, premium-grade sand, and reclaimed water that returns to our operations. This is not a side benefit of how we work; it is the centre of how we work, and the foundation of the environmental case we make to every Customer.

Safety First

Safety remains the foundation of everything we do at OE. In 2025, we deepened our investment in the systems and habits that protect our people. We maintained both our ISO 45001 and COR 2020 certifications through the surveillance audits completed during the year and continue to operate as a Recognized Safe Employer under the Chief Prevention Officer’s Supporting Ontario’s Safe Employers (SOSE) program — a recognition we have not previously highlighted, but which speaks to the standard we hold ourselves to.

We saw a significant increase in front-line leadership presence in the field, including a 41% year-over-year increase in job site audits. We also introduced bi-weekly incident reviews that bring front-line leadership together to learn from incidents and identify meaningful preventative actions. Safety performance is now directly linked to management incentive pay — a deliberate signal that this work is core to the business, not adjacent to it.

Our zero lost-time injury goal remains in place. We did not achieve it in 2025, and that result is one we sit with honestly. It is the reason the work continues.

Using AI-Enabled Tools

Our AI-enabled fleet camera system delivered measurable behavioral change in 2025. Across the program, we recorded an 80% reduction in handheld device use, an 80% reduction in seatbelt non-compliance, and a 52% reduction in triggered safety events. In total, 162 drivers received coaching across 611 sessions — coaching led by people, supported by data.

We extended AI applications into sales support, estimating, and contract review during the year. Looking ahead, our plan is to use AI-enabled tools to support our dispatch process. Our approach to AI is straightforward: it should make our people safer, faster, and more effective at the work they already do well. Technology serves the team, not the other way around.

Growing Closer to Our Customers

In 2025 we did not open new regional cities, but we expanded our Ottawa yard to better support Customer demand. We also grew our service footprint with Enbridge Gas across South-Western Ontario, Barrie, and the Greater Toronto Area, and began expanding our work with electricity distribution utilities across those same regions — a meaningful diversification of our Customer base. Our Storm and Underground Service Engineering (SUE) and Enbridge Gas Inc. (EGI) work expanded across the board.

Growth in 2025 was about serving existing Customers better — adding capacity, depth, and responsiveness in the regions where we already operate.

The Power of LEAN Thinking

LEAN remains how we improve, every day. In 2025, our teams completed 204 continuous improvement initiatives through our Quick and Easy Kaizen program — a number that represents real participation across the company, not a corporate target. We also ran a three-day cross-functional Kaizen with significant field crew involvement on our Asset Management Program (AMP) work, which delivered a 20%-plus gain in throughput. That is what LEAN looks like at OE: structured discipline, applied by the people closest to the work.

New Partnerships and Indigenous Engagement

Our commitment to deepening engagement with Indigenous businesses and communities continued in 2025. Building on the foundational work begun in 2024 — joining the Canadian Council for Indigenous Business, attending conferences, and engaging an Indigenous Relations Advisor — we used 2025 to develop a multi-year action plan and to begin training at the leadership level. Our approach is field-first and evidence-based: we are building this program to be operational, measurable, and owned by OE for the long term. We will say more about this in the People and Culture section of this report.

Our Vision and Values

Our vision — to provide groundbreaking utility support services, driven by unparalleled safety and sustainability to help our communities thrive — remains unchanged for 2025. Our four core values — Respect Each Other, Be Responsive, Champion Safety, and Do The Right Thing — remain unchanged as well.

What changed in 2025 was how we use them. We have woven the values more deliberately into recognition, tying praise directly to the value being demonstrated. Values become real when they show up in the language of everyday work.

Building on Our Progress

Looking ahead, three commitments anchor our forward view:

  • We will continue to grow and expand our hydrovac and ancillary services, with a focus on serving Customers in the regions where we already operate.
  • We will continue to advance innovation in safety and operational technology, with discipline and measured deployment.
  • We will continue to earn the trust of our Customers — every day, on every job site.

2026 also marks an important milestone for OE: our 30th anniversary. Thirty years of utility support work across Ontario, built on the same foundation we report against today — safety, environmental responsibility, the people who do the work, and the trust of the Customers we serve. We will recognize the anniversary throughout the year, including a monthly leadership article series that gives voice to the people who have built this company and continue to carry it forward.

Thank you for your continued support and trust in OE Utility Services. The work is the work, and we will do it together.

Environmental Practices

Driving Change with Sustainable Practices

Hydrovac waste processing at OE’s integrated soil recycling facility

Minimizing Environmental Impact

OE continues to operate with environmental responsibility as a core operating principle. Our 2.5-hectare hydrovac waste disposal and processing site continues to operate under the approval of the Ministry of the Environment, Conservation and Parks, in compliance with all applicable Ontario environmental regulations. Our Environmental Compliance Approval remains current, as are our obligations under Ontario Regulation 406/19 and the City of Vaughan’s sanitary discharge requirements. No regulatory inspections, orders, or correspondence of concern were received during 2025.

In 2025, our integrated soil recycling facility began a comprehensive plant overhaul scheduled for completion in the first quarter of 2026. The overhaul is an investment in the capacity, reliability, and long-term performance of the facility that anchors OE’s environmental case — and a signal of our continued commitment to upgrading rather than relying on what is already in place.

How we manage hydrovac waste defines who we are. OE disposes only at sites operating under a Ministry of the Environment, Conservation and Parks Environmental Compliance Approval (ECA) — and the bulk of the material we collect is processed at our integrated soil recycling (ISR) facility, where it is transformed into usable, saleable products: clean fill, premium-grade sand, and reclaimed water. Our soil handling and refining processes continue to enable 100% of hydrovac waste to be upgraded for value-added use. This is not a marginal practice at OE; it is the operating model.

How refining works

Refining Helps Everyone

Refining is not just a process; it is a commitment to keeping valuable materials in productive use rather than sending them to landfill. When hydrovac slurry arrives at our ISR facility, every drop of water is repurposed — supporting the upgrading process from treatment through to refilling our hydrovac trucks. The material itself is transformed into commercially saleable products that return to the construction supply chain: clean fill, premium-grade sand for utility pipe bedding, and reclaimed water. The refining process itself remains unchanged in 2025 — proven, controlled, and applied consistently across our operations. All materials continue to undergo rigorous testing, tracking, and tracing to monitor contamination risk.

Premium Performance Pipe Bedding

Our recycled sand continues to be formulated for use as premium bedding material for underground utility piping. Engineered for high performance with higher moisture content, our bedding material experiences less sinkage and fewer effects from freeze-thaw contraction. The product specification and process are unchanged for 2025 — what is proven, we keep.

Doing More to Address Climate Change

Our facility-level climate practices remain in place in 2025:

  • Facility warm-air redirect continues to heat freezing-sensitive outdoor operations on discharge.
  • High-efficiency, motion-sensing LED lighting remains in use throughout our facility.
  • Rainwater captured from our roof continues to be diverted into our soil recycling facility.
  • Office paper reduction and recycling remain active practices.
  • Approximately 60% of our 2025 vehicle purchases included biobased products in place of petroleum-based equivalents.

These are not commitments awaiting action — they are operating practices that continue to deliver results every day, in the background of how we run the business.

Purpose-Built, Environmentally Friendly Equipment

One of the most direct ways OE addresses environmental impact is through the equipment we choose to operate. Our hydrovac fleet is purpose-built — selected and specified to deliver high productivity with reduced fuel consumption, lower noise output, and reduced emissions relative to standard alternatives.

Our fleet now numbers 183 vehicles, an increase from 2024. Approximately 60% of our fleet has now been converted to vegetable-oil hydraulic systems, further reducing environmental risk in the event of a leak or spill. The technical advantages our equipment was selected for in 2024 — quieter operation in noise-sensitive areas, cold-weather cabinetry that eliminates freeze-up, rotating water nozzles for safer and faster excavation, the highest flow rates in the industry, and dig reach up to 900 feet from the truck — remain current in 2025.

OE’s purpose-built hydrovac fleet — now numbering 183 vehicles across six Ontario locations

Bundling Services for a Greener Tomorrow

At OE, our integrated service model continues to be one of the most direct ways we reduce environmental impact for our Customers. By combining hydrovac excavation, road cutting and coring, U-Fill services, aggregate and sand delivery, surface restoration, support vehicles and crews, and soil disposal and recycling under one company, we eliminate the inefficiency, the redundant travel, and the coordination overhead that come with multiple subcontractors.

The environmental case for bundling is simple: fewer trucks on the road, less repeated travel between sites, and more material reused in place of disposed. The operational case is equally clear: faster projects, lower coordination cost, and a single point of accountability for safety and quality.

Making Sustainable Connections with Our Customers

Our co-location model — positioning our operations close to where the work is — continues to be a defining feature of how OE delivers value. Co-location reduces travel, reduces emissions, and reduces cost. It also keeps our crews closer to home and closer to the communities we serve.

In 2025, we continued to serve Customers from our six locations across Ontario — the Head Office and Main Yard in Vaughan, and regional yards in Hamilton, Durham, Barrie, London, and Ottawa. The model remains the foundation of our regional growth strategy, and the principle behind it — bring the work, the people, and the materials together — continues to guide how we plan operations.

Accident Prevention and Driver Training

Reviewing fleet-camera coaching data — AI supports the coaching; people lead it

Commitment to Indigenous Engagement

Commitment to Indigenous Engagement

Meaningful Indigenous relations begin with respect, understanding, and a genuine commitment to creating mutual benefit. This commitment is a direct reflection of our core values. Operating across diverse regions of Ontario, we acknowledge that our work takes place on the traditional territories of many First Nations communities, including the Anishinaabe, Haudenosaunee, Huron-Wendat, and Mississauga peoples. We are grateful for the opportunity to live and work on these lands.

In 2024, we began a deliberate journey to deepen our engagement with Indigenous businesses and communities — joining the Canadian Council for Indigenous Business, participating in key conferences, and engaging an Indigenous Relations Advisor. We approached that work with a clear principle: learn before acting and challenge our assumptions through thoughtful questions before making promises we have not yet earned.

In 2025, we continued that learning posture while taking concrete steps forward. We delivered foundational training on Indigenous histories, cultures, and contemporary realities to our Executive Leadership Team — a deliberate choice to start at the top. Leaders who do not understand cannot lead a program that does. Building on that foundation, we developed a multi-year action plan to guide OE’s Indigenous engagement going forward — one that is field-first, evidence-based, owned by OE, and built for the long term rather than for any single moment of communication. We also began the transition to a consulting partner with deeper sector experience, positioning OE to move from foundational learning to operational delivery in the work ahead.

We continue to operate from a position of honesty about where we are. We do not yet have active partnerships with Indigenous businesses in the utility support services sector. We do not yet have measurable procurement spend with Indigenous-owned businesses. We do not yet have the metrics framework we committed to in 2024. These are not things to obscure — they are the next steps of the work, and they will be reported when there is real progress to report.

Member, Canadian Council for Indigenous Business

OE joined the CCIB in 2024 as a foundational step in our Indigenous engagement, gaining access to its programming, network, and certified Indigenous business directory.

Our forward commitments remain:

  • Extending Indigenous histories, cultures, and contemporary realities training beyond the Executive Leadership Team to broader audiences within OE.
  • Pursuing meaningful partnerships with Indigenous businesses in our sector, where genuine business alignment exists.
  • Establishing a metrics framework that allows us to track progress and hold ourselves accountable in future reports.

Building trust takes time and consistent action. As we move forward, we will continue to listen, learn, and adapt our approach — and we will report what we do, not what we intend to do.

Driving Greenhouse Gas Reduction Using LEAN Thinking Principles

Environmental Stewardship

At OE, we believe every business has a meaningful role in environmental stewardship. We are committed to reducing greenhouse gas emissions and partnering with Customers who place environmental responsibility at the centre of their operations.

We continue to meet or exceed all legislative requirements for the safe testing, handling, and disposal of hydrovac waste under provincial Regulation 406/19. Our Environmental Compliance Approval (ECA) remains current, and we continue to meet the City of Vaughan’s sanitary discharge requirements.

Collaborative Efforts for a Sustainable Future

Greenhouse gas reduction at OE is driven by the operational choices that define how the company runs: LEAN discipline that eliminates waste at every step, a co-location model that reduces vehicular travel, on-premises soil disposal and recycling that eliminates long-distance haul, integrated service bundling that reduces the number of trucks required to complete a project, and a steady investment in equipment specified for lower fuel consumption and emissions. These are the levers we control directly, and they are the levers we continue to apply.

OE is currently undertaking a comprehensive review of our greenhouse gas measurement methodology and baseline assumptions, with the goal of reporting against a robust, defensible framework in future reports. We will provide an updated position on our long-term emissions trajectory once that work is complete.
