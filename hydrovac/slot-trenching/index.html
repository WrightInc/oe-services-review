The Process

What Slot Trenching Is

Slot trenching — also called narrow trenching — uses pressurised water and a high-flow vacuum to cut a clean, narrow trench exactly where it's needed, removing only the soil the work requires.

Because the trench is narrow and precisely controlled, the surrounding ground stays largely intact. That means a smaller footprint to open, tighter control around existing utilities, and far less surface to put back once the new line is in.

Locates first: Because slot trenching is a form of excavation, valid locates are required before work begins. OE manages this end-to-end, including Ontario One Call submissions.

Why OE

The Integrated Difference

Most providers cut the trench and move on. OE cuts the trench — and stays on site for the install, the tie-in, the backfill, and the restoration that follow.

That single-source model keeps narrow-trenching projects moving: the same crew that opens the trench can place conduit or pipe, backfill, and restore the surface — same contract, same project lead. Customers get one point of accountability, drawing on 70+ trucks and 6 Ontario locations.

Bundling the trench with the work on either side of it is where OE's LEAN approach pays off — up to 10% cost savings and 50% productivity gains, plus a $40/hr discount when services are booked together. Spoil is hauled to OE's own ECA-approved central GTA soil recycling facility, cutting travel costs and emissions by 30%, and our NextGen hydrovacs run 10dB quieter for residential and noise-sensitive sites.
