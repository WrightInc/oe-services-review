The Method

What Hydrovac Power Distribution Excavation Is

Hydrovac power distribution excavation across Ontario uses pressurised water to break up soil and a high-flow vacuum to lift the slurry away, exposing buried electrical infrastructure without the blunt-force risk of a backhoe or trencher.

For power distribution specifically, the stakes are different from any other utility. A mechanical strike on an energized cable is not only a costly outage — it is an arc-flash and electrocution hazard to the crew on site.

That is why OE treats this work by a clear principle: safer than the alternative is not the same as safe. Hydrovac is the right method around distribution plant precisely because it removes the strike risk — but it is still excavation near energized infrastructure, and we treat it with full hazard controls, not casual confidence.

Locates first. Every excavation begins with valid locates. Under Ontario Regulation 213/91 (Construction Projects), s. 228, the employer must obtain a valid locate and choose a digging method that prevents contact with a buried utility before any ground disturbance. Notify the utility owner directly or through Ontario One Call. Locates are generally valid about 30 days (per the joint Electrical Safety Authority (ESA) / Technical Standards and Safety Authority (TSSA) guideline). Where energized electrical plant is present, hand digging or vacuum excavation is the practice referenced in ESA guidance, which points to the Infrastructure Health and Safety Association (IHSA) SPG4 for detailed safe-work procedures around underground electrical utilities.
Applications

Power Distribution Excavation Applications

🎯

Daylighting Distribution Cable

Exposing buried primary and secondary distribution cable for inspection, condition assessment, and tie-in work.

📍

Potholing & Depth Verification

Test holes to confirm the horizontal and vertical position of conduit, duct banks, and direct-buried cable before drilling or trenching.

⚡

Excavation Near Energized Plant

Controlled exposure adjacent to live distribution infrastructure where mechanical digging is prohibited or unsafe.

🪜

Pole & Anchor Foundations

Excavating holes for power poles, light standards, and anchors in congested or surfaced areas.

🧱

Duct Bank & Conduit Exposure

Uncovering concrete-encased duct banks and conduit runs for repair, extension, or crossing work.

🔌

Transformer & Switchgear Pads

Precise excavation around pad-mounted equipment without disturbing connected cable.

🏭

Substation Support Excavation

Non-destructive digging within substation footprints where cable congestion and grounding make mechanical excavation high-risk.

🚨

Emergency & Fault Locating Support

Rapid, controlled exposure to support fault-finding and restoration on distribution circuits.
