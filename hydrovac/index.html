Common Questions

Hydrovac Excavation FAQ

Answers to the questions OE's sales team hears most often.

What is hydrovac excavation?
Hydrovac excavation uses pressurized water to safely loosen soil while a high-flow vacuum extracts the slurry into a debris tank. The result is a precise, non-destructive method of digging — particularly valuable around buried utilities where mechanical excavation risks costly damage.
How does hydrovac compare to traditional mechanical excavation?
Hydrovac is significantly safer, faster in most applications, and dramatically reduces utility strikes — the leading cause of expensive jobsite incidents. Mechanical excavation remains appropriate for some bulk earthworks, but for any work near buried utilities, hydrovac is the industry standard.
Does OE handle winter and cold-weather hydrovac work?
Yes. Our fleet uses heated water and cold-weather cabinetry designed to operate through Ontario winters without freeze-up. Hydrovac is one of the few excavation methods that remains viable through deep frost conditions.
What size jobs does OE take on?
Everything from single-pothole utility locates to multi-month municipal infrastructure programs. With 70+ trucks across six Ontario locations, OE scales to match project scope without subcontracting.
How does OE handle disposal of excavated material?
Through our MOECP-approved soil recycling facility in the Central GTA. Hydrovac slurry is sorted, tested, and processed into reusable products — including premium pipe-bedding sand. Over 100,000 tonnes have been diverted from landfill to date.
What separates OE from other Ontario hydrovac providers?
Most hydrovac companies provide trucks. OE provides integrated utility infrastructure service — excavation, utility work, restoration, and disposal under one contract, one team, one point of accountability. That single-source model is what 30 years of Ontario municipalities, utilities, and ICI clients have built relationships around.
How quickly can OE respond to emergency calls?
Our 24/7 dispatch line is 416-749-0005. GPS-tracked fleet positioning allows real-time routing to emergency sites across our six Ontario locations. Response times vary by location but our regional coverage model is designed to put a truck on-site as fast as anyone in the province.
