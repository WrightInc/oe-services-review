The Process

What Daylighting Is

Daylighting is the controlled exposure of buried utilities — bringing pipes, cables, and conduit to "daylight" so they can be visually verified before further excavation proceeds.

OE crews use pressurised water to liquefy soil while a high-flow vacuum extracts the slurry — no mechanical tool ever contacts the asset.

Ontario regulatory note: Under O. Reg. 213/91, hydrovac daylighting meets the definition of an excavation, which means valid locates are required before work begins. OE manages this end-to-end, including Ontario One Call submissions.

Why OE

The Integrated Difference

Most hydrovac providers expose the utility and leave. OE exposes the utility — and stays on site for whatever comes next.

That single-source model matters most when daylighting reveals a problem: damaged insulation, corroded pipe, undocumented cross-bore, leak indication. With OE, the same crew that exposed the issue can mobilise the repair, replacement, or restoration — same contract, same project lead.

Bundling the exposure with the repair or restoration that follows — one mobilization instead of a second crew called back — is where OE's LEAN approach pays off: up to 10% cost savings and 50% productivity gains, plus a $40/hr discount when services are booked together. Spoil is hauled to OE's own ECA-approved central GTA soil recycling facility, cutting travel costs and emissions by 30%, and our NextGen hydrovacs run 10dB quieter for residential and noise-sensitive sites.
