The Process

What Contaminated Soil Removal Is

When soil on a site is impacted — by hydrocarbons from an old fuel tank, by historical industrial use, by a spill — it has to come out and go somewhere it's allowed to go. The challenge is rarely the digging itself. It's removing the impacted material precisely, without dragging clean soil into the disposal stream, and without striking the utilities and structures that tend to run through exactly the areas that need remediation.

This is where non-destructive excavation earns its place. High-pressure water breaks up the impacted soil and a high-capacity vacuum lifts the slurry straight into a contained debris tank — no spoil pile spreading across the site, no mechanical bucket tearing through buried lines, and a removal footprint that can be held to the area that actually needs it. Taking out only what has to come out keeps disposal volumes — and the cost and liability that ride with them — as low as the job allows.

From the tank, the material is hauled by a licensed carrier to a facility approved to receive it. OE handles the excavation and the compliant haulage as one scope, coordinated with the Customer's environmental consultant or qualified person, who directs the testing, classification, and where the material is permitted to go.

Where OE fits: OE is the precision-excavation and licensed-haulage partner on a contaminated-soil job — not the environmental consultant. Classifying the soil, assessing the site, and filing the records Ontario requires are the work of a qualified person under the Customer's direction. OE removes the material precisely and moves it compliantly, working to that plan. One scope, clear lanes.
Locates first: Impacted soil is often most concentrated around the buried tanks, lines, and structures that caused or contained it. Before any excavation, we work to confirmed locates. Non-destructive removal is the method that protects those structures and the crew — but safer than the alternative is not the same as safe. The locate comes first.
